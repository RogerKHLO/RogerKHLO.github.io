The Brief

The objective was to engineer the mechanical skeleton for a "Smart Tool Storage and Dispensing Machine." The system needed to automatically retrieve and return tool baskets without human oversight, requiring a rigid, 3-axis gantry system capable of handling a 5kg dynamic payload.

Key constraints included preventing "back-driving" (dropping the load) during power loss, ensuring smooth horizontal translation across storage racks, and precise linear actuation for tray insertion.

The Arsenal

The mechanical architecture utilizes a modular V-slot aluminium framework driven by a hybrid configuration of DC and Stepper motors.

Worm Gear DC Motor
The Hoist (Z-Axis). Selected for its self-locking properties, ensuring the platform holds its vertical position even when unpowered.
Planetary Gear Motor
The Drive (X-Axis). A JGB37-555 motor providing high torque to move the 8kg vehicle mass across the horizontal rails.
NEMA 17 Stepper
The Hand (Y-Axis). Drives a GT2 belt system for the push-pull mechanism, offering precise position control for tray engagement.
Electromagnet
The Grip. A 12V end-effector capable of securing 3kg metal baskets during the retrieval cycle.
2020 V-Slot Profile
The Skeleton. Provides a modular, rigid, and lightweight frame that supports gantry wheels and custom 3D-printed brackets.

The Execution

Z-Axis Hoisting: Safety was paramount. I calculated the static hold requirement at 49.05N. A standard motor would fail under gravity, so I implemented a 5840-31ZY Worm Gear motor. The worm drive mechanism inherently locks the output shaft, acting as a mechanical brake.

X-Axis Drive: To move the 8kg gantry assembly, I engineered a friction-drive system using a Planetary Gear Motor. Calculations determined a required peak torque of 0.28 Nm to overcome rolling resistance and accelerate to 0.1 m/s within 0.8 seconds.

Y-Axis Actuator: For the delicate task of extracting trays, I designed a linear actuator using a NEMA 17 stepper and GT2 belts. This setup delivers 31 RPM with sufficient torque to overcome the 14.7N static friction of the loaded trays.

The Roadmap

While the current mechanical system is fully operational, the following upgrades have been identified to enhance robustness and scalability:

  • Suspension Drive System: Implementing a compliant suspension mount for the drive wheel to ensure consistent rail contact and reduce structural loading on the main joints.
  • Encoder Integration: Replacing the multi-sensor photo-interrupter array with a rotary encoder system. This would reduce cabling complexity and allow for infinite vertical positioning resolution.
  • Passive Alignment Guides: Adding physical "landing spots" or grooves on the rails to passively self-center the platform at each column, reducing the reliance on perfect motor accuracy.
  • Soft Robotics Gripper: Exploring compliant soft-robotic end-effectors or suction mechanisms to replace the electromagnet, offering more versatile handling for non-ferrous tool baskets.

The Result

The fabricated system successfully achieved coordinated XYZ motion. The Z-axis hoist eliminated "back-driving" risks, and the modular chassis allowed for precise sensor mounting. The mechanical subsystem provided a stable, rigid foundation for the electronic integration and automated control logic.
